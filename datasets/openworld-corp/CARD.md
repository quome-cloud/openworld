# Dataset card — openworld-corp

## Summary
LLM-generated, state-grounded corporate transcript corpus (meetings + Slack) for a
synthetic DigitalOcean-style PaaS company, used as the perception layer of a
composite company world model (E48).

## Provenance
- **Organization**: synthetic (`org.py`) — fictional company "Nimbus Cloud", five
  divisions, ~21 named (fictional) employees, hand-set ground-truth metrics. No
  real person or company is depicted.
- **Text**: generated by a local LLM (default qwen2.5:7b via Ollama) from grounded
  prompts in `generate_corpus.py`. The committed `corpus.json` is a real sample
  from that generator. Regenerable with any `BaseLLM` (local or hosted API).

## Intended use
- Driving / testing a corporate world model's perception (recovering division
  health from meeting prose), and the E48 experiments (individual-vs-collective
  optimization, value-of-action by org level, perception cost, navigation policy).
- A template: swap `org.py` for a real org, or replace `corpus.json` with real
  transcripts (same schema) to run on an actual company.

## Grounding / state-recoverability
Every record carries the `ground_truth` metrics it was generated from, so the
prose is recoverable: a `TextPerceptor` extracts division metrics back out, and
extraction fidelity is exactly what the perception experiment measures.

## Limitations & ethics
- Synthetic and stylized; not a real company's data and not a calibrated business
  simulator. Metrics and people are invented.
- LLM-generated text can contain artifacts/repetition and reflects the generator
  model's biases; transcripts are illustrative, not ground-truth meeting records.
- On real organizational data, transcripts and Slack are sensitive: handle under
  the appropriate consent, retention, and access controls; this dataset ships only
  synthetic text to avoid that.
